This project involved designing and implementing an Information Security Management System (ISMS) aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 for a mid-size financial services organization. The engagement covered gap analysis, risk assessment, policy development, control implementation, and readiness for third-party certification audit.

ISO 27001 is the internationally recognized standard for ISMS. Achieving certification demonstrates an organization's commitment to managing information security risks systematically and continuously.

  • Identified internal and external issues (ISO 27001 Clause 4.1)
  • Mapped interested parties and their requirements (Clause 4.2)

Risk Assessment & Treatment

ISO 27001 mandates a formal information security risk assessment process. We used a qualitative approach aligned with ISO 31000, scoring likelihood and impact on a 1–5 scale, producing a risk heat map for executive reporting.

Risk Treatment Options
Mitigate

Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact below the acceptance threshold.

Transfer

Shift risk via cyber insurance or contractual obligations to third parties.

Accept

Document accepted risks with senior management sign-off where cost exceeds benefit.

Avoid

Cease the activity that introduces the risk where operationally feasible.

// 05

Control Implementation

Selected controls from Annex A were implemented based on the risk treatment plan. Each control was assigned an owner, implementation timeline, and evidence requirement for audit purposes.

Key Technical Controls Deployed
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all privileged and remote access accounts
  • Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR) deployed across 100% of managed endpoints
  • Data Loss Prevention (DLP) rules applied to email and cloud storage platforms
  • Patch management SLA: Critical — 24h, High — 7 days, Medium — 30 days
  • Network segmentation with firewall rules reviewed and tightened
  • Encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3) enforced
Statement of Applicability (SoA)

The SoA documented all 114 Annex A controls, whether each was included or excluded, and justification. Included controls referenced the specific risk or legal/contractual requirement driving their selection.

Note

The SoA is a mandatory deliverable for ISO 27001 certification and must be reviewed and approved by senior management before the Stage 2 audit.

Lessons Learned

Key takeaways from the implementation that will strengthen future ISMS programs:

  • Engage senior leadership early — ISMS success depends on visible management commitment
  • Integrate ISMS into existing change management and project governance processes
  • Asset inventory completeness was the biggest time sink — automate with CMDB from day one
  • Conduct tabletop exercises during implementation, not after — staff awareness gaps surface early
  • Risk re-assessment should be scheduled quarterly, not just annually
