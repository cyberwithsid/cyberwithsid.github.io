A comprehensive suite of Incident Response (IR) playbooks covering the most common and impactful security incident types. Each playbook provides analysts with structured decision trees, step-by-step response procedures, escalation criteria, containment actions, and evidence collection checklists.

Playbooks are aligned with NIST SP 800-61 Rev 2 (Computer Security Incident Handling Guide) and map response phases to PICERL: Preparation, Identification, Containment, Eradication, Recovery, and Lessons Learned.

Playbook 1 – Ransomware

CRIT

Ransomware is a Severity 1 incident. Activate the IR team immediately and notify management within 30 minutes of confirmation.

Detection Indicators
  • Mass file rename events with unknown extensions detected by EDR/FIM
  • Shadow copy deletion commands: vssadmin delete shadows /all /quiet
  • Lateral movement alerts: PsExec, WMI, or SMB scanning
  • C2 beacon to known ransomware infrastructure IPs/domains
Containment Steps
bash — Immediate Containment 
# Step 1: Isolate affected endpoint at network level
netsh advfirewall set allprofiles firewallpolicy blockinbound,blockoutbound

# Step 2: Disable the compromised AD account immediately
Disable-ADAccount -Identity "compromised.user"

# Step 3: Snapshot affected systems before wiping
aws ec2 create-snapshot --volume-id vol-xxxxx --description "IR-snapshot-$(date +%F)"

Playbook 3 – Insider Threat

Insider threats require a HR-coordinated response with strict need-to-know handling. Evidence must be preserved forensically and legal counsel engaged early.

Warn

Do NOT alert the suspected insider during investigation. Premature notification can lead to evidence destruction. Loop in Legal and HR before any confrontation.

  • Preserve logs from SIEM, DLP, endpoint, badge access, and cloud storage
  • Request legal hold on all data sources within 24 hours of escalation
  • Conduct forensic imaging of the suspect's workstation — do not power off
  • Review 90-day data transfer history (USB, email, cloud sync)
  • Prepare chain of custody documentation for all evidence

Playbook 4 – Data Exfiltration

Data exfiltration incidents trigger regulatory notification obligations under GDPR, HIPAA, and other frameworks. Time-to-notification SLAs make speed critical.

RegulationNotification WindowTo WhomTrigger
GDPR72 hoursSupervisory AuthorityPersonal data breach
HIPAA60 daysHHS + IndividualsPHI breach ≥500 records
PCI DSSImmediateCard brands + AcquirerCHD compromise
UAE PDPL72 hoursData OfficePersonal data breach
Note

Start the notification clock from the moment you have reasonable certainty of a breach, not from when forensic analysis is complete. Document every decision and timestamp.
